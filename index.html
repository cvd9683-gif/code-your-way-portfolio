Social Interaction Design Mobile

ForkTales

A social cooking app that uses daily prompts, team challenges, and shared feasts to turn everyday meals into moments of connection.

RoleProduct Designer
Duration10 Weeks
ToolsFigma, Canva

Context & Role

Cooking Much Alone

ForkTales was born out of a pressing observation: designed for millions, our cooking apps fail to motivate and shared interest in trying together makes cooking effective and more intentional while keeping people on track in a productive mission.

As the sole product designer, I was responsible for the entire design — from initial sketches and prototypes to high-fidelity screens and the final pitch. The goal was to make ForkTales feel warm, playful, and built for real humans — not productivity machines.

How might cooking feel different if it were treated as a shared experience, not a solo task?

Design Focus

Three Design Pillars

Playful

Cooking feels lighter when it's framed as a game. Prompts, streaks, and celebratory moments make the routine feel special.

Shared

Cooking is more fun together. Team challenges and ingredient sharing create genuine social rituals around food.

Rewarded

Progress is visible. Completing challenges and sharing feasts earns points and unlocks community recognition.

Key Features

Feature 1: Cooking Prompts

Notification DesignDaily Engagement

Daily prompts serve as a gentle nudge that pulls you out of your usual rotation. They invite something new rather than something you're assigned to.

Feature 2: Team Assignment & Home Feed

Social DesignCommunity

After downloading, users are assigned to real teams and brought into an existing shared kitchen where they feel like they're joining something already in motion.

Feature 3: Team Feasts

SocialRitualCommunity

Users cook the same dish at the same time and bring their results to a virtual feast. The app celebrates the attempt, not just the outcome.

Outcome & Reflection

ForkTales taught me that cooking is a deeply social act that technology had mostly turned into a solo one. It reinforced my belief in designing systems that encourage people rather than optimize them.

Next Steps

  • Explore how team dynamics shift when group sizes vary (2 vs. 8 people)
  • Test whether prompts work better as push notifications or in-app moments
  • Add dietary accommodations to ensure accessibility for all team members
  • Build out official advisor connections so teams can get professional guidance

Web Design AI Healthtech B2B

Clinic Catalyst Website

A website designed to clearly introduce and communicate a medical AI platform for clinics, investors, and clinical stakeholders.

RoleProduct Design, Inc.
Duration6 Weeks
ToolsFigma

Context & Role

Meet Clinic Catalyst

Clinic Catalyst is an AI-powered platform designed to reduce administrative burden for clinics and streamline interactions between clinics and hospitals.

I joined the team of startup designers tasked with designing a marketing website for the company. The challenge was communicating their identity to a wide range of audiences — from clinicians to investors.

In healthcare and investor settings, unclear storytelling can make strong products feel less trustworthy.

Design Approach

01 —

Competitor Analysis

I started by reviewing available website options to identify product and healthcare tech competitors, breaking them down into common structural patterns.

02 —

Refining the Message Structure

Working alongside the founders, I organized the platform's message into a clear narrative arc with each page section serving a specific communication purpose.

03 —

Wireframing & Modular Design

I developed and evolved wireframes for the non-core breakdowns hierarchy, focusing on flexibility and finding the right fit for each module.

04 —

Visual Direction & Iteration

The visual direction intentionally bridged clinical credibility with approachability, developing the visual language while translating values into something recognizable.

05 —

Motion & Storytelling

I explored animation patterns and motion principles to create micro-interactions that guide users through the platform's value proposition without overwhelming them.

Outcome

The result was a purpose-built experience for medical and clinical healthcare content. Clinic Catalyst now has a visual voice, along with a series of components aligned with bringing the visual language to life.

Next Steps

  • Explore interactive prototype testing with clinical stakeholders
  • Develop component library documentation for the broader design team
  • Test content hierarchy with investors vs. clinical audience segments

Product Design Mobile App UX Research

Trader Goe's

A mobile app concept focused on faster grocery planning, discovery, and delivery for Trader Joe's shoppers.

RoleProduct Designer
Duration3 Weeks
ToolsFigma

Context & Role

Grocery Shopping, Simplified

Trader Joe's has always been an experience — the products, the culture, the discovery. But without a dedicated app to support planning, delivery, or discovery, shopping could feel disjointed.

As the sole product designer, I led feature concept research, user interview synthesis, low-fidelity wireframes, and validated design decisions used in the final deliverable.

How might Trader Joe's app extend the in-store experience to better support planning, discovery, and convenience?

Design Focus

The project focused on three people who shop at Trader Joe's often, all with very different changing experiences. I prioritized speed, flexibility, and content discovery while staying true to the brand's playful, community-driven feel.

Key Features

Feature 1: Recipe Discovery & Filters

Product DiscoveryNavigation Design

Users can browse recipes using quick tag-based filters that map to mood topics and preferences, making it easier to decide what to cook and buy in a single flow.

Feature 2: Integrate Podcast Experience

ParticipatingSocial Integration

Trader Joe's podcast content built directly into the app with a listening bar that stays accessible while users browse products or check out.

Feature 3: Live Order Tracking

Trust BuildingCore Service

A real-time order status and delivery tracking helps users feel confident about their orders and have a clearer picture of when groceries will arrive.

Outcome & Reflection

The final result combines delivery, planning and podcasts into one experience. The project pushed me to think carefully about scope and difficulty while designing within a strong brand identity.

Next Steps

  • Explore more robust features and interactions to be tested with potential users
  • Continue to find user needs and test more features that fit their lifestyle
